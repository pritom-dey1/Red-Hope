Quick Answers

Who can donate blood?
Any healthy person aged 18–65 with a minimum weight of 50 kg can donate blood. Certain medical conditions may prevent donation temporarily or permanently.
How often can I donate blood?
You can donate whole blood every 3–4 months. Platelet or plasma donations have different intervals. Always follow guidelines given at the donation center.
What happens after I donate blood?
You will rest for a few minutes, receive refreshments, and be monitored for any side effects. Most donors can resume normal activities immediately.
Is blood donation safe?
Yes, blood donation is safe. Sterile, single-use equipment is used, and the process is monitored by trained medical staff.
What should I do before donating blood?
Eat a healthy meal, stay hydrated, avoid alcohol, and get enough rest. Bring a valid ID to the donation center.
